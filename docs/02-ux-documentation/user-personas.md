# User Personas

## Project: AnchorAbroad - Visa Counseling Platform

---

## Persona 1: Sarah Chen - Graduate Student Applicant

![Professional Woman](../assets/persona-sarah.jpg)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 24
- **Location:** Shanghai, China
- **Education:** Bachelor's in Computer Science
- **Occupation:** Recent graduate
- **Income:** Dependent on family

### Goals
- Obtain student visa for Masters program in USA
- Navigate visa application process successfully
- Ensure all documents are correct before submission
- Meet university deadlines for enrollment

### Frustrations
- Overwhelmed by complex visa requirements
- Worried about making mistakes on application
- Uncertain about supporting documents needed
- Concerned about visa interview preparation
- Limited knowledge of US immigration policies

### Technology Profile
- **Devices:** Smartphone (primary), laptop
- **Tech Savvy:** High
- **Preferred Channels:** Mobile apps, email, video calls
- **Social Media:** WeChat, Instagram, LinkedIn

### Behavior Patterns
- Researches extensively online before making decisions
- Seeks recommendations from friends who studied abroad
- Prefers step-by-step guidance
- Values expert advice and personalized support
- Willing to pay for quality service

### User Journey
1. Searches online for visa counseling services
2. Reads reviews and testimonials
3. Explores website to understand services
4. Registers account to start application
5. Fills out preliminary assessment
6. Books consultation session
7. Completes full visa application with guidance
8. Tracks application status
9. Prepares for interview with counselor support

### Needs from Platform
- Clear, easy-to-follow application process
- Mobile-friendly interface
- Expert guidance at each step
- Document checklist
- Quick response to questions
- Status tracking
- Interview preparation resources

### Quote
> "I just want someone who has done this before to tell me I'm on the right track. The visa process is too important to mess up."

## Persona 2: Rajesh Kumar - Skilled Worker Applicant

![Professional Man](../assets/persona-rajesh.jpg)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 32
- **Location:** Bangalore, India
- **Education:** Bachelor's in Engineering, MBA
- **Occupation:** Software Engineer (5 years experience)
- **Income:** $25,000/year

### Goals
- Obtain H-1B work visa for US tech company
- Relocate family to United States
- Advance career with international experience
- Secure better opportunities for children's education

### Frustrations
- Limited time due to full-time job
- H-1B lottery system uncertainty
- Complex legal requirements
- Dependent visa process for spouse and children
- Previous rejection creates anxiety

### Technology Profile
- **Devices:** Laptop (work), smartphone
- **Tech Savvy:** Very high (software engineer)
- **Preferred Channels:** Email, web portal, professional networks
- **Social Media:** LinkedIn, Twitter, WhatsApp

### Behavior Patterns
- Prefers efficient, streamlined processes
- Values data security and privacy
- Expects professional, enterprise-grade service
- Makes decisions based on success rates and data
- Limited availability during work hours

### User Journey
1. Researches visa success rates and expert credentials
2. Compares multiple counseling services
3. Checks for employer sponsorship support
4. Creates account during evening hours
5. Completes application in multiple sessions
6. Uploads documents from desktop
7. Schedules consultations around work schedule
8. Monitors application progress regularly

### Needs from Platform
- Quick, efficient process (respects time)
- Ability to save progress and return later
- Secure document upload and storage
- Evening/weekend consultation availability
- Clear timeline and milestones
- Mobile app for quick status checks
- Professional, business-like communication

### Quote
> "I need a service that understands I'm working full-time. I can't afford to miss this H-1B season."

## Persona 3: Maria Rodriguez - Family Reunification Applicant

![Professional Woman Smiling](../assets/persona-maria.jpg)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 45
- **Location:** Mexico City, Mexico
- **Education:** High school diploma
- **Occupation:** Small business owner
- **Income:** $15,000/year

### Goals
- Obtain visitor visa to see daughter in USA
- Navigate process despite limited English
- Prove strong ties to home country
- Avoid visa rejection

### Frustrations
- Not confident with English language
- Intimidated by government forms
- Previous visa denial creates fear
- Limited computer skills
- Uncertainty about interview questions

### Technology Profile
- **Devices:** Smartphone (primary)
- **Tech Savvy:** Low to medium
- **Preferred Channels:** Phone calls, WhatsApp, in-person
- **Social Media:** Facebook, WhatsApp

### Behavior Patterns
- Seeks personal, empathetic support
- Values human connection over automation
- Needs extra explanation and reassurance
- Prefers voice/video over written communication
- Relies on family for technology help

### User Journey
1. Daughter finds service and recommends it
2. Daughter helps create account
3. Prefers phone consultation over forms
4. Fills forms with counselor guidance (video call)
5. Sends documents via WhatsApp or mail
6. Practices interview with counselor
7. Receives encouraging messages before interview

### Needs from Platform
- Simple, clear language (Spanish support ideal)
- Large, easy-to-read interface
- Minimal typing required
- Phone/video support availability
- Patient, understanding counselors
- Step-by-step verbal guidance
- Interview preparation focused on her situation

### Quote
> "I'm not good with computers, but my daughter said you would help me. I just want to visit my grandchildren."

## Persona 4: James Thompson - Business Visa Applicant

![Professional Man in Suit](../assets/persona-james.jpg)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 38
- **Location:** London, UK
- **Education:** MBA from top university
- **Occupation:** Business Development Director
- **Income:** $80,000/year

### Goals
- Obtain B-1 business visa quickly
- Attend critical client meetings in USA
- Maintain professional image throughout process
- Minimize time spent on application

### Frustrations
- Unpredictable travel schedule
- Can't afford delays or rejections
- Needs white-glove service
- Expects immediate responses
- Impatient with bureaucracy

### Technology Profile
- **Devices:** iPhone, MacBook Pro, iPad
- **Tech Savvy:** High
- **Preferred Channels:** Email, SMS, video calls
- **Social Media:** LinkedIn (professional only)

### Behavior Patterns
- Expects premium, concierge-level service
- Willing to pay more for expedited service
- Delegates tasks to assistant when possible
- Values efficiency and professionalism
- Judges service by responsiveness

### User Journey
1. Searches for premium visa services
2. Evaluates based on turnaround time
3. Has assistant create account and fill basics
4. Does final review and approval
5. Expects counselor to handle maximum details
6. Pays premium for priority processing
7. Receives reminders for any required actions

### Needs from Platform
- VIP/priority support tier
- Expedited processing options
- Calendar integration for scheduling
- Email/SMS notifications
- Minimal user input required
- Professional, corporate aesthetic
- Account delegation to assistant

### Quote
> "I travel for business constantly. I need someone who can handle the visa details so I can focus on my clients."

## Design Implications

### For Sarah (Graduate Student)
- Mobile-first design
- Step-by-step wizards
- Progress indicators
- Educational content
- Checklist features

### For Rajesh (Skilled Worker)
- Save and resume functionality
- Desktop-optimized
- Document management
- Timeline views
- Email notifications

### For Maria (Family Reunification)
- Large, clear buttons
- Minimal text input
- Phone call scheduling
- Video consultation
- Multi-language support

### For James (Business Executive)
- Premium service tier
- Concierge features
- Calendar integration
- Delegate access
- Priority support

---

## Usage Scenarios by Persona

These personas represent the primary user segments for AnchorAbroad. Features should be prioritized based on serving the needs of Sarah and Rajesh (highest volume), while ensuring Maria and James also have positive experiences.
