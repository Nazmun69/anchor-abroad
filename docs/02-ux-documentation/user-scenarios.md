# User Scenarios

## Project: AnchorAbroad - Visa Counseling Platform

---

## Scenario 1: First-Time Student Visa Application

**Persona:** Sarah Chen (Graduate Student Applicant)  
**Context:** Sarah has been accepted to a Masters program and needs to apply for an F-1 student visa  
**Frequency:** Once per user  
**Priority:** High

### Pre-conditions
- Sarah has received university acceptance letter
- Sarah has valid passport
- Application deadline is 3 months away
- Sarah has never applied for US visa before

### Scenario Flow

#### Step 1: Discovery
Sarah searches Google for "student visa counseling services" and finds AnchorAbroad. She reads the homepage, views testimonials from other students, and decides to try the service.

#### Step 2: Registration
She clicks "Apply Now" but is redirected to the registration page. She creates an account using her email address and receives a verification email.

#### Step 3: Email Verification
Sarah checks her email, clicks the verification link, and is redirected back to the site with a confirmed account.

#### Step 4: Initial Assessment
After logging in, Sarah is guided to complete a preliminary assessment that asks about her visa type, purpose, timeline, and background. The system provides her with an initial recommendation and estimated timeline.

#### Step 5: Application Form
Sarah navigates to the visa application form and begins filling out her information:
- Personal details (name, DOB, nationality, passport number)
- Contact information (email, phone, current address)
- Education background (current degree, intended program)
- Financial information (funding source, amount available)
- Visa specifics (F-1 type, study purpose, expected duration)

The form auto-saves her progress as she types.

#### Step 6: Review and Submit
After completing all sections, Sarah reviews her information, makes a few corrections, and submits the application. She receives a confirmation message and sees her submission in the dashboard.

#### Step 7: Dashboard Monitoring
Over the next few days, Sarah checks her dashboard to see if the counselor has reviewed her application. The status changes from "Pending" to "Under Review."

#### Step 8: Follow-up
Sarah receives guidance on next steps via email and continues to use the platform for document preparation and interview practice.

### Success Criteria
- Sarah completes registration in under 3 minutes
- Verification email arrives within 30 seconds
- Application form is completed in one session (15-20 minutes)
- Sarah feels confident about her submission
- Dashboard clearly shows application status

### Alternative Paths
- **Path A:** Sarah starts the form but doesn't finish. She returns the next day and continues from where she left off (saved progress).
- **Path B:** Sarah encounters a field she doesn't understand. She uses the help icon to get clarification.
- **Path C:** Sarah tries to submit without completing required fields. She receives clear validation errors.

### Pain Points Addressed
- Simplified, step-by-step process reduces overwhelm
- Auto-save prevents data loss
- Clear validation prevents submission errors
- Status tracking reduces anxiety

## Scenario 2: Busy Professional Applying for Work Visa

**Persona:** Rajesh Kumar (Skilled Worker Applicant)  
**Context:** Rajesh needs to apply for H-1B visa while working full-time  
**Frequency:** Once per user, with possible renewals  
**Priority:** High

### Pre-conditions
- Rajesh has job offer with US company willing to sponsor
- Rajesh works 9am-7pm and has limited free time
- H-1B filing deadline is approaching
- Rajesh has compiled most required documents

### Scenario Flow

#### Step 1: Research Phase
During lunch break, Rajesh researches visa counseling services on his phone. He bookmarks AnchorAbroad to review later on his laptop.

#### Step 2: Evening Registration
At 9pm after work, Rajesh opens the site on his laptop and creates an account. He wants to complete everything efficiently.

#### Step 3: Partial Application
Rajesh starts filling the visa application form but realizes he needs some documents from his employer. He saves his progress at 10pm and logs out.

#### Step 4: Document Collection
Over the next two days, Rajesh collects required documents during work breaks. He keeps them in a folder on his laptop.

#### Step 5: Application Completion
On Saturday morning, Rajesh logs back in and sees his partially completed application. He continues from where he left off, uploads documents, and completes the form.

#### Step 6: Document Upload
Rajesh uploads:
- Passport scan
- Degree certificates  
- Employment offer letter
- Resume
- Previous visa documents

Each upload shows a progress bar and confirms successful upload.

#### Step 7: Submission and Payment
Rajesh reviews all information, submits the application, and sees it appear in his dashboard with "Pending Review" status.

#### Step 8: Mobile Monitoring
During the week, Rajesh checks his dashboard on his phone during commute to see status updates. He receives email notifications when status changes.

#### Step 9: Consultation Scheduling
When the counselor reviews his application, Rajesh receives an email to schedule a consultation. He books an evening slot that fits his work schedule.

### Success Criteria
- Rajesh can save progress and return later
- Document uploads work smoothly from desktop
- Mobile dashboard shows real-time status
- Evening consultation slots are available
- Email notifications keep him informed without constant checking

### Alternative Paths
- **Path A:** Rajesh tries to access on phone but prefers desktop experience for form filling
- **Path B:** One document fails to upload; clear error message explains file size/format issue
- **Path C:** Rajesh wants to edit application after submission; system allows editing before counselor review

### Pain Points Addressed
- Save/resume functionality respects limited time
- Multi-device support (desktop + mobile)
- Email notifications reduce need for constant checking
- Flexible consultation scheduling accommodates work schedule

## Scenario 3: Non-Technical User with Language Barrier

**Persona:** Maria Rodriguez (Family Reunification Applicant)  
**Context:** Maria needs visitor visa to see daughter but has limited English and computer skills  
**Frequency:** Occasional (every few years)  
**Priority:** Medium

### Pre-conditions
- Maria's daughter lives in USA
- Maria has been denied visa once before
- Maria is nervous about the process
- Maria's English is limited; Spanish is primary language
- Maria primarily uses smartphone

### Scenario Flow

#### Step 1: Daughter's Assistance
Maria's daughter discovers AnchorAbroad and creates an account for her mother, entering basic information.

#### Step 2: Initial Contact
The daughter schedules a phone consultation in Spanish for Maria. The counselor calls Maria directly.

#### Step 3: Phone Assessment
During the 30-minute call, the counselor:
- Asks questions about Maria's situation
- Explains the visa process in simple Spanish
- Reassures Maria about previous denial
- Schedules follow-up video call

The counselor enters Maria's responses into the system.

#### Step 4: Document Collection
The counselor sends Maria a simple WhatsApp message with a list of documents needed:
- Passport
- Bank statements
- Property documents
- Business registration
- Family photos

Maria's daughter helps her scan documents using smartphone.

#### Step 5: Video Consultation
Maria joins a video call with the counselor who:
- Reviews documents together
- Practices interview questions
- Explains what to expect at embassy
- Addresses Maria's concerns
- Builds Maria's confidence

#### Step 6: Application Submission
The counselor completes the online application form based on information gathered. Maria only needs to review and approve via video call.

#### Step 7: Interview Preparation
Leading up to the interview, Maria receives encouraging WhatsApp messages. She has a final practice session via video call.

#### Step 8: Post-Interview
After the interview, Maria calls the counselor to share results. The counselor provides support regardless of outcome.

### Success Criteria
- Maria never feels frustrated with technology
- Maria understands the process in her language
- Maria feels supported and confident
- Daughter can monitor progress on behalf of mother
- Personal touch makes Maria feel valued

### Alternative Paths
- **Path A:** Maria prefers in-person meeting; counselor arranges local partner
- **Path B:** Maria can't use video calls; phone calls and WhatsApp suffice
- **Path C:** Maria's daughter wants to attend consultations; system allows guest participants

### Pain Points Addressed
- Minimal technology requirement (mostly phone/WhatsApp)
- Spanish language support
- Personal, empathetic service
- Family member can assist/monitor
- Interview preparation reduces anxiety

## Scenario 4: Executive Needing Expedited Service

**Persona:** James Thompson (Business Visa Applicant)  
**Context:** James needs urgent business visa for client meeting in 2 weeks  
**Frequency:** Multiple times per year  
**Priority:** Medium

### Pre-conditions
- James has unexpected business trip to USA
- Meeting is critical for major deal
- James expects premium service
- James's assistant handles initial logistics

### Scenario Flow

#### Step 1: Urgent Search
James's assistant searches for "expedited visa services" and finds AnchorAbroad's premium tier. She notes the 48-hour turnaround promise.

#### Step 2: Assistant Registration
The assistant creates an account on behalf of James, marking it as "Corporate/Premium" account. She enters James's basic information.

#### Step 3: Priority Support
The assistant immediately receives a call from a senior counselor offering white-glove service. They discuss James's situation and urgent timeline.

#### Step 4: Information Gathering
The counselor sends a concise list of required information to the assistant:
- Passport details
- Business purpose letter
- Company registration
- Previous visas
- Meeting invitation

#### Step 5: Expedited Processing
The assistant uploads documents and completes the application during her work hours. She marks it as "Urgent - Premium Service."

#### Step 6: Fast-Track Review
A senior counselor reviews the application within 2 hours and schedules a 15-minute video call with James for the next morning.

#### Step 7: Executive Consultation
James joins the brief video call during his lunch break. The counselor:
- Confirms application details
- Explains expedited embassy appointment process
- Provides interview talking points
- Answers James's questions efficiently

Total call time: 12 minutes.

#### Step 8: Embassy Coordination
The counselor arranges an emergency embassy appointment within 3 days. James receives calendar invitation automatically.

#### Step 9: Interview Prep
James receives a concise one-page brief via email with key points to remember. No lengthy video required.

#### Step 10: Follow-up
After the interview, James's assistant receives status update. Visa is approved in time for the trip.

### Success Criteria
- Response time under 2 hours
- Minimal time required from James personally
- Assistant can handle most interactions
- Professional, corporate-level service
- Expedited timeline met

### Alternative Paths
- **Path A:** James prefers to handle personally; counselor adapts to his preference
- **Path B:** Documents are incomplete; counselor guides assistant on exactly what's missing
- **Path C:** Embassy has no appointments; counselor explores alternative solutions

### Pain Points Addressed
- VIP treatment matches expectations
- Delegation to assistant possible
- Minimal time investment required
- Expedited processing available
- Professional communication style

## Scenario 5: Returning User - Visa Renewal

**Persona:** Rajesh Kumar (returning after 3 years)  
**Context:** Rajesh needs to renew his H-1B visa  
**Frequency:** Every 3 years  
**Priority:** Medium

### Pre-conditions
- Rajesh used AnchorAbroad for initial H-1B
- Rajesh's information is stored in system
- Rajesh has new employer (H-1B transfer)
- Rajesh remembers positive previous experience

### Scenario Flow

#### Step 1: Return Visit
Rajesh receives email reminder that his visa expires in 6 months. He logs into his existing AnchorAbroad account.

#### Step 2: Previous Application Review
Rajesh sees his dashboard showing his previous successful application from 3 years ago. A banner suggests "Need to renew? Start here."

#### Step 3: Pre-filled Renewal Form
Rajesh clicks "Renew Application" and sees a new form pre-filled with his previous information:
- Personal details (same)
- Passport information (updated)
- Contact details (address changed)
- New employer information (needs update)

#### Step 4: Update Information
Rajesh only needs to update:
- New employer name and details
- Current address
- Upload new offer letter
- Upload updated resume

This takes 5 minutes instead of 20 minutes.

#### Step 5: Express Submission
Rajesh submits the renewal application. The system recognizes him as a returning user and assigns the same counselor who helped him before.

#### Step 6: Familiar Counselor
The counselor reviews and sends a personalized message: "Great to work with you again! I see you've changed employers. Let me review the transfer requirements."

#### Step 7: Streamlined Process
Because Rajesh is familiar with the process, consultation is brief (10 minutes) focusing only on what's different for the renewal.

#### Step 8: Successful Renewal
Rajesh's renewal is processed smoothly. He recommends AnchorAbroad to a colleague who is also applying.

### Success Criteria
- Previous data is accessible and reusable
- Renewal process is faster than initial application
- Returning users feel recognized and valued
- Same counselor provides continuity
- Recommendation/referral feature available

### Alternative Paths
- **Path A:** Rajesh forgot password; password reset is quick and secure
- **Path B:** Rajesh wants different counselor; system allows selection
- **Path C:** Rajesh refers colleague; both receive referral benefits

### Pain Points Addressed
- Data reuse saves time
- Continuity with same counselor
- Recognition as valued returning customer
- Streamlined renewal process
- Easy referrals

## Cross-Scenario Insights

### Common Success Patterns
1. **Clear Communication:** All scenarios emphasize transparent status updates
2. **Flexibility:** Users appreciate multiple ways to interact (web, mobile, phone, video)
3. **Personalization:** Service adapts to user's tech comfort and language
4. **Time Respect:** System saves progress and minimizes time investment
5. **Confidence Building:** Support reduces anxiety throughout process

### Critical User Needs
- **Trust:** Professional, credible service
- **Clarity:** Understand what's needed and what happens next
- **Control:** See status and manage own timeline
- **Support:** Access help when needed
- **Security:** Know data is protected

### Design Priorities
1. Mobile-responsive design (Sarah, Maria)
2. Save/resume functionality (Rajesh)
3. Multi-language support (Maria)
4. Clear status tracking (all personas)
5. Flexible communication channels (all personas)
6. Document management (Rajesh, James)
7. Service tiers (James vs. Sarah)
